Blockchain and Wal-Mart

You may be wondering, what does Blockchain and Wal-Mart have to do with each other? 

Recently I read a story, that Wal-Mart had interest in Bitcoin and buying Bitcoin. And then I ran across another article that there were rumors that Wal-Mart was going to announce their stake into Bitcoin. I knew that once Wal-Mart announces their stake in Bitcoin, then a lot of people who were skeptical of Bitcoin, were no longer going to be as skeptical. Currently, electric currency is still a very confusing topic and therefore, may scare them and don’t believe it’s worth any investment.

Based on my research with Bitcoin for the past 3 years, I have reason to believe Wal-Mart is working at bringing Bitcoin to the common person instead of just tech people or Bitcoin-ers who know what it is and how to trade. 

Then I came across a source code for Elon Musks Blockchain and thought to myself “this is something I can do.” So I conveniently searched up how to create Blockchain using Javascript. 

Most of the code was similar to what I had learned at Flatiron School with the exceptions of using a crypto-js npm package and also require(‘crypto-js/sha256’); 

We have the constructor, which is pretty straightforward and it creates instances of a class into objects. SHA256 creates a unique 32-byte hash for each block of the blockchain.
